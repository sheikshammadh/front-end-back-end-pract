What is organic food?

Organic food refers to products grown and processed without synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, genetically modified organisms (GMOs), or artificial additives. Organic farming practices aim to promote ecological balance, conserve biodiversity, and maintain soil health. As a result, organic food is often considered more environmentally friendly and can be perceived as healthier due to fewer chemical residues. Organic products can range from fruits and vegetables to dairy, meat, and packaged goods, all certified to meet organic standards.
